Projects and Experiences:

Triboelectric Nanogenerator

Day 1

During the first day of our lab project, my group focused on designing a Triboelectric Nanogenerator (TENG) device for our chosen customers, campers. We identified their need for a reliable light source on windy nights when a campfire is impractical. After brainstorming concepts like a wind-powered fan with triboelectric blades that generates a current to light the bulb, we finalized a more innovative design: an aluminum ball housed within a 3D-printed vortex whistle, utilising the wind to move around. Inside this chamber, the ball will roll between two walls (one coated in wool and the other in styrofoam) to generate a triboelectric charge through contact and separation. With our concept solidified, we concluded the day by beginning the CAD model in SolidWorks, preparing for 3D printing.
